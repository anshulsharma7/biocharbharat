The Problem

Every Year, India Burns
Its Own Future

At harvest season, millions of Indian farmers burn crop stubble — the cheapest way to clear fields. The result is one of the world's worst annual pollution events.

500M+

Tonnes of crop residue burned every year

Punjab, Haryana, UP alone account for 35M+ tonnes of paddy straw incinerated each harvest season.

AQI 999

Delhi air quality during peak burning

Farm fires cause 25% of Delhi's winter PM2.5 pollution. 200+ million people breathe hazardous air for weeks.

1.67M

Indians die from air pollution annually

Stubble burning is a major seasonal contributor. Farmers themselves breathe smoke 10–15x above safe limits.

₹50,000 Cr

Soil nutrients lost every season

Burning destroys nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium in topsoil — forcing farmers to spend more on fertilizers.

98,000+

Satellite-detected fire hotspots per season

NASA and ISRO satellites record tens of thousands of active farm fires across North India every October–November.

Zero

Scalable alternatives for farmers today

Manual stubble removal costs ₹4,000–6,000/acre. Most smallholders simply can't afford not to burn.

Root Cause

No Time

The window between paddy harvest and wheat sowing is just 2–3 weeks. Burning clears fields in hours. No alternative is that fast.

No Money

Mechanical clearing costs ₹4,000–6,000 per acre. A typical 2-acre smallholder earns ₹40,000–60,000 per season. The math doesn't work.

Policy Without Incentive

Governments ban burning but offer no profitable alternative. Fines are unenforceable at scale across millions of farms.

🔄

No Market for Residue

Crop residue has zero economic value to the farmer today. Until someone pays for it, nothing changes.

The Solution

Waste In. Wealth Out.
Carbon Down.

Biochar Bharat creates a farmer-powered carbon removal network — turning waste into income, soil health, and verified carbon credits.

STEP 01
🌾

Collect Residue

Farmers collect paddy straw, husks, and stubble that would otherwise be burned. We provide logistics support.

→ Waste gets value
STEP 02
⚗️

Pyrolyse into Biochar

Residue is converted in low-cost kilns through pyrolysis — controlled oxygen-limited burning that locks carbon.

→ Carbon is captured
STEP 03
🌱

Apply to Soil

Biochar is mixed into farmland, improving water retention, soil nutrients, and yield — permanently.

→ Soil health improves
STEP 04
💰

Earn Carbon Credits

We aggregate verified carbon removal across thousands of farms, sell credits to global buyers, pay farmers directly.

→ Farmer earns income
